Shwe Yin Aye

Shwe Yin Aye is a classic Burmese dessert soup often enjoyed during festivals and hot weather.
It blends coconut milk, sweet jellies, bread pieces, and chilled tapioca for a creamy, refreshing treat.

Ingredients

  • Coconut milk
  • Pandan-flavored jelly or agar agar
  • Shredded coconut
  • Sago pearls or tapioca
  • Sweetened condensed milk
  • Bread pieces or caramelized bread
  • Evaporated milk
  • Ice cubes or crushed ice

The Process: The Art of Making Shwe Yin Aye

  1. Cook the sago pearls until translucent, then rinse under cold water.
  2. Prepare pandan jelly or agar agar and cut into small squares.
  3. Soak the bread pieces in a little coconut milk until soft.
  4. Combine coconut milk, evaporated milk, and sweetened condensed milk in a bowl.
  5. Add the jellies, sago, shredded coconut, and soaked bread, then serve over ice.
